Here is a list of common types of portfolio services along with an explanation for each:

Portfolio Managemen

Portfolio management involves the professional management of investment portfolios on behalf of clients. Portfolio managers make strategic investment decisions based on the client's financial goals, risk tolerance, and time horizon.

Financial Planning

Financial planning services help individuals create comprehensive financial plans that include investment portfolios, retirement planning, risk management, estate planning, and tax strategies. Financial planners work with clients to achieve their financial goals.

Wealth Management

Wealth management services cater to high-net-worth individuals and families, providing a holistic approach to managing their finances. Wealth managers offer investment management, financial planning, estate planning, tax services, and other personalized financial advice.

Discretionary Portfolio Services

Discretionary portfolio services give portfolio managers the authority to make investment decisions on behalf of clients without requiring their approval for each transaction. This service is suitable for clients who prefer a hands-off approach to managing their investments.

Fund Management

Fund management services involve managing mutual funds, hedge funds, or other investment funds on behalf of investors. Fund managers make investment decisions to achieve the fund's objectives and maximize returns for investors.

Advisory Portfolio Services

Advisory portfolio services offer personalized investment advice and recommendations to clients, but the final investment decisions rest with the client. Advisors provide guidance on asset allocation, investment selection, and portfolio rebalancing based on the client's financial goals.
